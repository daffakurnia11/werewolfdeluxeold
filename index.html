What is Werewolf?

Werewolf is a simple game for a large group of people (seven or more). It requires no equipment besides some bits of paper, you can play it just sitting in a circle. I would call it a party game, except that it's a game of accusations, lying, bluffing, second-guessing, assassination, and mob hysteria.
- Andrew Plotkin

How To Play?

Werewolf is a game that can be played with a large group of people. The object of the game is to identify and kill the werewolves amongst the villagers. Start by shuffling and dealing out the role cards. There are two main sides and other side, the two sides are ‘Good Side’ and ‘Bad Side’. In this game making sure to include, a Villager, Two Werewolves, a Bodyguard, and a Seer card. The game is divided into two stages. A night phase and a day phase. Then, the night phase begins and the moderator has the werewolves choose a victim, the Bodyguard is allowed to save one person, and the Seer gets to guess at one person they suspect of being a werewolf. When the night round ends, the day round begins and the players discuss their characters and then take a vote to execute on who they believe is a werewolf. That player is then killed and the night round begins again. The game continues until either the good side or the bad side or maybe the other side win.
